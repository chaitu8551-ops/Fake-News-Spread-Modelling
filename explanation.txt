================================================================
        INFODEMIC WATCH — FULL MODEL EXPLANATION
        SIR Misinformation Propagation on Barabasi-Albert Networks
================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. OVERVIEW
----------------------------------------------------------------
Infodemic Watch simulates how misinformation spreads through a
social network using an extended SIR (Susceptible–Infected–
Recovered) epidemiological model. Instead of a biological virus,
the "infection" here is a piece of false information or rumour.

The simulation runs on a Barabasi-Albert (BA) scale-free network,
which closely mimics the structure of real social networks such as
Twitter, Facebook, and WhatsApp — where a small number of highly
connected "hub" users dominate information flow.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE NETWORK — BARABASI-ALBERT (BA) MODEL
----------------------------------------------------------------
A BA network is built using preferential attachment:
  - New nodes are added one at a time.
  - Each new node connects to m existing nodes.
  - Nodes with more connections are more likely to receive new links.

This produces a scale-free network with a power-law degree
distribution — a few hubs have very high connectivity while most
nodes have only a few connections. This mirrors real social media
platforms.

Parameters:
  N  — Total number of nodes (users) in the network.
  m  — Number of edges each new node attaches to (controls density).
       Higher m = denser network = faster spread.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE FIVE STATES
----------------------------------------------------------------
Each node in the network is assigned one of five states:

  [S] SUSCEPTIBLE (Blue)
      A regular user who has not yet encountered the misinformation.
      They can become Believers if exposed by a neighbour.

  [I] BELIEVERS (Red)
      An infected user who believes the misinformation and actively
      spreads it to their neighbours. This is the "infectious" state.

  [R] RECOVERED (Green)
      A user who previously believed the misinformation but has since
      corrected their belief (debunked, lost interest, or self-corrected).
      Recovered users do not spread misinformation further.

  [SK] SKEPTICS (Purple)
      Users who are inherently resistant to misinformation. They have
      a reduced probability of becoming Believers when exposed.
      They model critically thinking or media-literate users.

  [FC] FACT-CHECKERS (Amber)
      Specialised nodes that accelerate recovery in their neighbourhood.
      When a Believer has a Fact-Checker as a neighbour, their recovery
      rate (gamma) is multiplied by fc_mult, helping them recover faster.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. MODEL DYNAMICS — HOW EACH STEP WORKS
----------------------------------------------------------------
At every time step, the following rules are applied simultaneously
to all nodes:

  SPREADING (S -> I or SK -> I):
    For each Believer node, it attempts to infect each susceptible
    or skeptic neighbour:
      - If neighbour is Susceptible:   infect with probability beta
      - If neighbour is a Skeptic:     infect with probability beta * sk_mult
        (sk_mult = 0.3 by default, meaning skeptics are 70% harder to infect)

  RECOVERY (I -> R):
    Each Believer recovers spontaneously with probability gamma.
    If the Believer has at least one Fact-Checker neighbour, the
    effective recovery rate becomes gamma * fc_mult (boosted).

  STATIC ROLES:
    Skeptics and Fact-Checkers never change state — they are fixed
    roles representing structural properties of users.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. KEY PARAMETERS
----------------------------------------------------------------
  beta  (Transmission rate)
        Probability that a Believer infects a Susceptible neighbour
        in a single time step.
        Range: 0.01 – 0.15
        Higher beta = faster, wider spread.

  gamma (Recovery rate)
        Probability that a Believer recovers (stops spreading) in
        a single time step.
        Range: 0.01 – 0.08
        Higher gamma = shorter infection duration.

  Steps (T)
        Number of simulation time steps to run.

  Init I %
        Percentage of susceptible nodes seeded as initial Believers
        at the start of the simulation.

  Skeptics %
        Fraction of the total population assigned as Skeptics before
        the simulation begins.

  FC %
        Fraction of the total population assigned as Fact-Checkers.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. INTERVENTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Two interventions can be toggled on and compared against a baseline:

  FC BOOST
        Activates an enhanced Fact-Checker effect. The FC multiplier
        (fc_mult, range 2x–4x) amplifies the recovery rate of
        Believers who are neighbours of Fact-Checkers.
        Models organised fact-checking campaigns or platform labels.

  REMOVE HUBS
        Removes the top hub_pct% most-connected nodes from the network
        before the simulation runs. This models:
          - Suspending or banning super-spreader accounts.
          - Algorithmic demotion of viral content.
          - Deplatforming high-reach users.
        Hub removal can dramatically reduce spread because hubs act
        as bridges between communities in scale-free networks.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. THE BASIC REPRODUCTION NUMBER (R0)
----------------------------------------------------------------
R0 is the average number of new Believers one Believer generates
in a fully susceptible population. It is calculated as:

        R0 = beta * <k> / gamma

where <k> is the average node degree (mean number of connections).

  R0 > 1 : The misinformation will spread exponentially (epidemic).
  R0 = 1 : Borderline — spread is self-sustaining but not growing.
  R0 < 1 : The misinformation will die out naturally.

R0 is shown in the Results tab for both Baseline and Intervention runs.

----------------------------------------------------------------
8. OUTPUT METRICS
----------------------------------------------------------------
  Peak I %
        The maximum percentage of the population that simultaneously
        believed the misinformation at any point in time.
        Lower is better — indicates the intervention flattened the curve.

  Final R %
        The percentage of the population that was ever infected and
        then recovered by the end of the simulation.
        This represents the total reach of the misinformation campaign.

  R0
        Basic reproduction number as defined above.

----------------------------------------------------------------
9. READING THE TIME SERIES CHART
----------------------------------------------------------------
The Time Series tab shows all five state populations (%) over time:

  Solid lines   = Baseline (no intervention)
  Dashed lines  = Intervention run

Watch for:
  - How quickly the red Believer curve peaks.
  - Whether the green Recovered curve plateaus low (contained) or high.
  - The gap between solid and dashed lines shows intervention effectiveness.
  - Purple (Skeptics) and Amber (Fact-Checkers) remain flat as they
    are fixed roles and do not change state.

----------------------------------------------------------------
10. READING THE NETWORK VISUALISATION
----------------------------------------------------------------
The Network tab shows the BA graph at its initial state (t=0).
Node colours match the five states listed above.
Hub nodes appear at the centre of clusters with many connections.
Use this to visually inspect how roles are distributed and how
central Fact-Checkers and Skeptics are placed.

----------------------------------------------------------------
11. REAL-WORLD INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------------------------------
This model is an abstraction. In practice:

  Skeptics          ~ Media-literate users, journalists, educated readers
  Fact-Checkers     ~ Verified fact-checking accounts, platform moderators
  Hub removal       ~ Account suspension, shadowbanning, viral content limits
  FC Boost          ~ Active debunking campaigns, warning labels on posts
  beta              ~ Virality of the content, emotional resonance
  gamma             ~ Natural correction rate, attention span decay

The model demonstrates that:
  1. Scale-free networks amplify misinformation far beyond random networks.
  2. Targeting hubs is more effective than broad suppression.
  3. Even a small % of Fact-Checkers can significantly reduce peak spread.
  4. Skeptics slow but do not stop an epidemic if R0 >> 1.
  5. Combining hub removal + FC boost produces the strongest containment.

----------------------------------------------------------------
12. LIMITATIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------
  - Nodes have fixed roles; in reality people change behaviour over time.
  - No re-infection: Recovered users cannot become Believers again.
  - The model is stochastic — results vary across runs (use seed for
    reproducibility).
  - Real networks have community structure not fully captured by BA graphs.
  - No external shocks (e.g., breaking news re-igniting spread) are modelled.
